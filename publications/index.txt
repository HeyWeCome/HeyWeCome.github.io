Existing methods typically involve two steps: first, using Pre-trained Language Models (PLMs) to convert user historical behaviors into semantic vectors; second, capturing latent user intentions through a behavioral sequence encoder. However, limitations still exist in this pipeline. Firstly, most studies directly fix the parameters of PLMs, making it difficult to optimize the generated semantic vectors for specific application scenarios. Even with fine-tuning PLMs considered, the huge model size brings computational burdens. To address this challenge, we introduce a Low-Rank Adaptation technique, which enhances the quality of semantic vectors by training two lightweight low-rank matrices while keeping the original parameters of PLMs frozen.
